Climate

Average temperature

The annual average temperature in the region is 22 to 26 °C (72 to 79 °F), with not much variation between the warmest and the coldest months. The hottest part of Brazil is the northeast, where temperatures of more than 38 °C (100 °F) are frequently recorded during the dry season between May and November.

 

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Brazil is from April to June, and August to October. Business visitors should avoid mid-December to the end of February, when it is hot and people are on holiday. In these months, hotels, beaches and transport tend to be very crowded. July is a school holiday month.

 

High season

generally speaking high season covers January, February and July during school holidays, although you may find high season prices at other times when there are public holidays in Brazil, special events or particularly good climactic conditions for a particular sport e.g. kitesurfing. 

 

Low season

Low or midseason is typically March after carnival until end June and then August to December 15, but not necessarily in cities. It is quite often the best time to go weather wise, although with 5 separate weather systems in the country it’s wise to check before you come!

 

Required clothing: 

·       As the weather is generally on the warmer side, bring clothes made from lightweight natural fabrics such as cottons and linens which you can layer up.

·       Waterproofs may be needed if visiting during the rainy season. Bring warm clothing if visiting the south during winter (June to August), whilst the extremely humid climate of the Amazon region demands specialist clothing for any treks or activity tourism.

·       Sunlight around the tropics is extremely strong and sunglasses are recommended.
